Dundee Law War Memorial

The 572-foot peak is the city's most distinctive landmark and an enduring attraction for visitors and locals. Dundee Law is a hill in the centre of Dundee, Scotland, and is the highest point in the city. The Law is what remains of a volcanic sill, which is the result of volcanic activity around 400 million years ago. With a large war memorial at its summit, it is the most prominent feature on the local skyline. The Law is a volcanic sill, despite the fact that everyone refers to it as "Dundee's Volcano." This is a subterranean, sideways lava flow that formed 400 million years ago when the rock was softer. Before harsh weather, rain, and glaciers removed the softer rock to reveal the hard volcanic rock that is visible today, the volcanic rock of Dundee Law was covered in layers of other rock for millions of years.

Dundee Law Timeline

3000 BC

Bronze Age, Local people have developed a complex society. Bronze Age people use the Law for rituals, including burying their dead with possessions such as weapons and clothing.

1500 BC - 100 AD

For a millennia, the Law served as a crucial component of Dundee's defenses. It was utilized as an Iron Age hillfort, and archaeological remains from around 1500 BC have been discovered on its inclines. Additionally, first-century AD Roman ceramics have been found.

1500s

Law re-fortified with Bastions, possibly during unrest during the Rough Wooing.

1629 - 1860 AD

To accommodate the Dundee to Newtyle railway, the Law had a 300-meter-long, 3-meter-diameter tunnel constructed through its eastern slope in the 1820s. The first locomotive, which was initially driven by horse, was unveiled in 1833 and continued to run until the 1860s, when a new railway route was constructed to avoid the hill.

1850s - 1908 AD

Open quarries produce high quality rock for construction. Quarries later used for land fill. The first car to drive to the top was a 1906 Werbell, built in Dundee.

16th May 1925 AD

The War Memorial is unveiled

1940s

World War 2, Pillbox built, memorial used as lookout, tunnel used as an air raid shelter.

1990s

Creating a Place for Visitors, View point built, archaeological excavations confirm existence of hillfort and bastions.
